<p><p>The Mobile Connected Smart Objects market can be categorized into two primary types: Direct Connectivity and Indirect Connectivity. </p><p>Direct Connectivity involves objects that connect directly to the internet or a mobile network, enabling real-time data exchange and control, such as smart thermostats and wearable devices. </p><p>In contrast, Indirect Connectivity refers to smart objects that connect through a gateway or hub, often using protocols like Zigbee or Z-Wave, which then link to the internet, as seen in home automation systems.</p></p>
